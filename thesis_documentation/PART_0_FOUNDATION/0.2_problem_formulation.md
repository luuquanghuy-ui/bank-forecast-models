# 0.2 Problem Formulation

## The Core Problem: Two Different Prediction Targets

Our models predict **two fundamentally different targets**:

| Model | Target | Scale | Meaning |
|-------|--------|-------|---------|
| **NeuralProphet** | Price | ~40-60 VND | Stock closing price tomorrow |
| **TFT** | Price | ~40-60 VND | Stock closing price tomorrow |
| **GARCH** | Volatility | ~0.01 | Expected |log return| tomorrow |
| **Hybrid** | Volatility | ~0.01 | Expected |log return| tomorrow |

**Critical Issue:** You CANNOT directly compare a price prediction model with a volatility prediction model.

---

## Why Price Prediction is Different from Volatility Prediction

### Example of the Scale Problem

```
NeuralProphet predicts: Price = 52 VND, Actual = 50 VND → MAE = 2
GARCH predicts: Volatility = 0.015, Actual = 0.012 → MAE = 0.003

MAE(2) > MAE(0.003)

Does this mean NeuralProphet is WORSE than GARCH?
→ NO! They predict DIFFERENT THINGS!
```

## Concept 1: Martingale Property (Why Price is Unpredictable)

### Definition
A stochastic process S_t is a **martingale** if:
```
E[S_{t+1} | I_t] = S_t
```

**Translation:** The best predictor of tomorrow's price is today's price.

### Implication for Our Problem
- If stock prices follow martingale, then:
  - E[price_{t+1} | price_t] = price_t
  - Any model trying to predict price will at best match naive
  - Complex models will OVERFIT noise and perform WORSE

### Evidence from Our Results

| Bank | Naive MAE (price) | NeuralProphet MAE (price) | TFT MAE (price) |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| BID | **0.46** | 1.25 (2.7x worse) | 1.27 (2.7x worse) |
| CTG | **0.34** | 0.66 (1.9x worse) | 5.27 (15.3x worse) |
| VCB | **0.58** | 1.64 (2.8x worse) | 2.32 (4.0x worse) |

**Conclusion:** Complex models (NP, TFT) are 90-300% WORSE than naive for price prediction. This proves martingale property holds for Vietnam bank stocks.

## Concept 2: Volatility Clustering (Why Volatility IS Predictable)

### Definition
Volatility clustering means:
- High volatility today → likely high volatility tomorrow
- Low volatility today → likely low volatility tomorrow

This is formalized in GARCH(1,1):
```
σ²_t = ω + α·ε²_{t-1} + β·σ²_{t-1}
```

### Why Volatility Persists
- α + β close to 1 → slow decay
- News arrives in clusters (good news followed by good news)
- Market participants react to volatility itself

### Evidence from Our Data

| Bank | α (ARCH) | β (GARCH) | α+β | Persistence |
|------|---------|----------|-----|-------------|
| BID | 0.059 | 0.920 | 0.979 | Very High |
| CTG | 0.090 | 0.888 | 0.978 | Very High |
| VCB | 0.076 | 0.886 | 0.962 | High |

**α+β ≈ 0.97** means volatility decays very slowly → predictable!

### Evidence from Our Results

| Bank | Naive MAE (vol) | GARCH MAE (vol) | Hybrid MAE (vol) |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|------------------|
| BID | 0.0114 | 0.0095 | **0.0080** |
| CTG | 0.0112 | 0.0097 | **0.0093** |
| VCB | 0.0093 | 0.0083 | **0.0066** |

**Hybrid wins by 17-30% over naive for volatility prediction!**

## The Fair Comparison Framework

### Cannot Compare Directly
```
NP (price) vs GARCH (volatility) = UNFAIR
```

### Must Compare Within Target

**For PRICE prediction:**
- Compare: Naive vs NP vs TFT
- Winner: Naive (martingale)

**For VOLATILITY prediction:**
- Compare: Naive vs GARCH vs Ridge vs RF vs Hybrid
- Winner: Hybrid (17-30% better than naive)

### Conversion for Fair Comparison

To compare NP/TFT (price models) with GARCH/Hybrid (volatility models), convert price predictions to volatility:

```
NP volatility = |log(NP_price_pred / NP_price_pred.shift(1))|
TFT volatility = |log(TFT_price_pred / TFT_price_pred.shift(1))|
```

This introduces additional error, so NP/TFT perform even worse when converted to volatility.
