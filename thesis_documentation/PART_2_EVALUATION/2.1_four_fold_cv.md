# 2.1 Four-Fold Walk-Forward Cross-Validation

## Why Four-Fold?

### Problems with Single Split
- Results depend on specific random split
- May not generalize to other time periods
- Cannot assess model robustness

### Solution: Four-Fold Walk-Forward
- Expanding window: each fold uses more data
- Tests on different time periods
- More reliable estimates of model performance

---

## Fold Structure

```
Fold 1: Train 50% | Val 15% | Test 15%
Fold 2: Train 65% | Val 15% | Test 15%
Fold 3: Train 80% | Val 15% | Test 15%
Fold 4: Train 90% | Val 5%  | Test 5%
```

### Why Expanding Window?
- Later folds have more training data
- More realistic: models improve over time
- Tests robustness across different data sizes

---

## Weight Selection Per Fold

For each fold, we find the best ensemble weight `w`:

```python
Hybrid = w × GARCH + (1-w) × Ridge
```

The weight is optimized on the validation set.

## Why Doesn't Hybrid Always Win?

### 1. Ridge Dominates in Calm Markets
- When market is stable, ML features work well
- Ridge alone can capture patterns
- GARCH contribution is minimal

### 2. GARCH Dominates in Volatile Markets
- When volatility clusters strongly, GARCH is essential
- Ridge features become less reliable
- Higher weight on GARCH helps

### 3. Weight Instability
- Optimal weight varies from 0.00 to 1.00
- This suggests the true optimal is somewhere in between
- Fixed 0.45 weight may be suboptimal in specific folds
