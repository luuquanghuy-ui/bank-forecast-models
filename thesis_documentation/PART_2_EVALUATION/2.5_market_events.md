# 2.5 Market Event Validation

## Purpose

Market event validation tests whether models perform well during:
- High volatility periods (top 20 highest vol days)
- Normal days

This is crucial because:
1. Volatility predictions are most valuable during volatile periods
2. Accurate risk assessment matters most in stress scenarios
3. Model failure during calm periods is less consequential

**Script:** `run_market_event_validation.py`
**Output:** `market_event_outputs/` (summary CSV + high vol days CSVs)

---

## Methodology

### Identifying High Volatility Days

1. Calculate daily |log return| for test period
2. Sort by volatility descending
3. Select top 20 highest volatility days
4. Compare model predictions vs actual on these days

### Validation Criteria

A good model should:
- Predict high volatility when volatility is high
- Not systematically under/over-predict during stress
- Have similar (or better) accuracy on high-vol days as on normal days

## Key Findings

### 1. TFT Dominates Overall Volatility

Across all banks and conditions:
- **TFT has the lowest overall volatility MAE** (0.0079-0.0080 vs others at 0.010-0.013)
- TFT reduces error by ~30% vs Naive on volatility
- TFT captures volatility clustering patterns best via attention mechanism

### 2. XGBoost/Hybrid Perform Well on High Vol Days

On the top 20 highest volatility days:
- **Hybrid** reduces error by 40-55% vs Naive on high vol days
- **XGBoost** reduces error by 32-41% vs Naive on high vol days
- **TFT** is best overall but not always best on high-vol specific days

### 3. Hybrid is Only Model That Beats Naive on Price

On both high vol and normal days:
- **Hybrid is the only model that beats Naive on price** (martingale property)
- XGBoost, NP are 200-1100% WORSE than Naive on price
- TFT is close to Naive but doesn't beat it
- This confirms: price is unpredictable (martingale), complex models overfit

### 4. Volatility Clustering Works

The reason models perform well on high vol days:
- Volatility clusters: high vol today → high vol tomorrow
- GARCH captures this with α+β ≈ 0.97 persistence
- Tree-based models (XGBoost) and attention (TFT) learn these patterns

### 5. Martingale Property Confirmed

On high vol days:
- Price prediction error is HIGH for all models
- Naive (last price) is competitive
- Only Hybrid (GARCH+Ridge) can slightly beat Naive

---

## Why TFT Dominates Volatility

### 1. Attention Mechanism
- TFT's attention captures temporal dependencies in volatility
- Multi-horizon learning better captures volatility clustering
- Quantile regression provides robust median prediction

### 2. Volatility Clustering
- High vol today → high vol tomorrow
- TFT's attention is specifically designed for temporal patterns
- Better than tree-based methods at capturing long-range dependencies

### 3. Comparative Results
- TFT: 0.0079-0.0080 overall volatility MAE
- XGBoost: 0.0105-0.0116 overall volatility MAE
- Hybrid: 0.0113-0.0121 overall volatility MAE
- NP: 0.0086-0.0130 overall volatility MAE
